UX/UI case study: Alffie’s website

If you were to go to Alffie’s website, you might have a hard time working out what they do, how they do it and who it’s for. Alffie are at the top of the employment education market and are trying to break into new markets, including providing technologies for corporate and government organisations. I believe the design of their current website and LMS could do a lot more to communicate that Alffie are a professional and successful organisation.


Solution

I put together a proposal to demonstrate the benefits and value UX/UI design can add to an organisation. I used Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and XD to show what an end product may look like.

Alffie’s website is currently a large mix of styles and colours, and there is no central theme or message. For my design, I drastically scaled back the amount of colours used, basing everything on a style guide that I created. I also developed some ideas around the style of copy I wanted. There should be strong calls to action and Alffie should quickly identify who they are, the problem they solve and how they do it.
