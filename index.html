Digital Product Design – UX/UI


Project Alffie’s website and learning management system (LMS).
Problem: A lack of cohesive product UX/UI design resulting in poor user experience, branding and professionalism. To put this another way, if you were to go to Alffie’s website, you would have a hard time working out what they do, how they do it and who it’s for. Alffie are at the top of the employment education market and are trying to break into new markets including providing technologies for corporate and government organisations. I believe the design of their current website and LMS do not communicate that Alffie are a professional and successful organistaion.
Solution: I put together a proposal to demonstrate the benefits and value UX/UI design can add to a organisation. I wanted to undo years of work without any UX/UI design or oversight. I put together a demo using Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop and XD of what an end product may look like.
Alffie’s website is messy and too colourful – there is no central theme or message. So I drastically scaled back the amount of colours used, basing everything on a style guide that I developed. I also developed some ideas around the style of copy I wanted. There should be strong calls to action and Alffie should quickly identify who they are, the problem they solve and how they do it.
